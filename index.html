Each cell is the region of floor space closest to one column — the area whose self-weight and live load that column carries. The result feeds column-sizing modules, footing design, and lateral-load distribution downstream.

01 · Geometric

Voronoi tessellation

Partitions a plane into regions where each region contains all points closest to a single seed. In structural engineering this derives load tributary areas from column or wall positions.

Used in STR · GEO · FOU
02 · Numerical

Finite element method

Discretizes continuous structural domains into a mesh of simple elements, then solves the partial differential equations governing stress, strain and displacement under load.

Used in STR · DYN · TIM · STL
03 · Optimization

Linear programming

Selects optimal profile dimensions from regulatory catalogs by minimizing material cost under load-capacity, deflection and serviceability constraints — guaranteed feasible solutions.

Used in STR · STL · TIM
04 · Logical

Constraint propagation

Maintains consistency across modules as inputs change. When the FEM module updates a beam, dependent modules — connections, footings, fire ratings — recompute automatically through the constraint graph.

Used platform-wide
05 · Spatial

R-tree spatial indexing

O(log n) geometry queries over tens of thousands of building elements. Without it, every "what intersects this zone" question would linearly scan the entire model. With it: instant.

Used in STR · GEO · FIRE · ARCH
06 · Integration

Gaussian quadrature

Computes loads over surfaces and volumes by numerical integration. Required for wind-pressure distribution, snow drift on roof geometry, and area-loaded live loads on irregular shapes.

Used in WIND · SNOW · STR

§ 01The ChallengeWhy Builtly exists

Engineering is locked in
manual workflows.

Consulting engineering is built on professional accountability, regulation and quality assurance — all correct. Beneath that lies a working pattern that has barely changed in twenty years: parallel tools, manual documentation, and an incentive structure that rewards hours over outcomes.

01Time allocation

Manual report production.

Engineers spend 40–60% of their time on documentation and report writing rather than engineering analysis. The result is lower productivity, longer delivery cycles, and the most expensive professional capacity in the industry deployed against the lowest-value tasks.

Tied up in paperwork40–60%
02Incentive structure

Established firms cannot self-disrupt.

When efficiency reduces billable hours, transformation is difficult to drive from within. Genuine reinvention rarely originates from the incumbents — it has to come from outside the existing fee model.

Status quoProfitable
03Senior capacity

Senior expertise is the bottleneck.

Markets globally lack senior structural, geotechnical, fire and acoustical engineers. Automating routine production frees expert capacity for work that genuinely requires professional judgment — where human accountability cannot be replaced.

BottleneckSenior, not software
04Tooling

Fragmented workflows.

Drawings in CAD. Calculations in spreadsheets. Reports in word processors. Requirements in separate sources. Every handoff is manual — and every manual handoff is a coordination problem, a delay, and a risk of error.

Tools per deliveryFour, minimum

§ 06ApproachWhat does not change

Builtly signs nothing.

Four principles that govern every module, every deliverable, every release. Software does not carry professional liability — engineers do.

  1. 01

    Rule-driven AI. Not open prose.

    7 jurisdictions · regulation engine

    AI operates within building codes, standards and regulatory frameworks defined as machine-readable logic. The model cannot freely interpret its way out of a compliance requirement.

  2. 02

    Deterministic first. AI last.

    Multi-pass · hard validation

    Geometry and physics are deterministic, computed exactly. AI describes the result and writes it up. Numbers do not come from a language model — they come from numerical engineering libraries running pure code.

  3. 03

    Human in the loop where it matters.

    Auto · Reviewed · Attested

    Qualified engineers with regulatory authority are integrated into the platform. Three levels of involvement. The signature on the report and the person liable for the work are always the same. AI is never the final approver.

  4. 04

    Open formats. No lock-in.

    IFC4 · DOCX · PDF · BCF

    IFC4, DWG, DXF, DOCX, PDF, BCF, GeoJSON. It goes in, it comes out. The client owns the result and can take it out of the platform the day it is signed.

Builtly provides the starting point. The engineer provides the engineering.
