§ 01PrinciplesWhat does not change

Three principles.

Trust on a platform that produces regulated engineering deliverables is not a feature — it is a precondition. Three principles govern every architectural decision below them.

§ Principle 01

Customer data stays customer data.

Project files, drawings, models and deliverables uploaded by a customer remain the customer's property and are not used to train any model. Data isolation is per-tenant at the storage layer.

§ Principle 02

Everything is auditable.

Every deliverable can be traced back to the exact inputs, the exact compute, the exact model version, and the exact engineer who signed. Compliance forensics is a property of the architecture, not a feature.

§ Principle 03

The engineer signs.

For deliverables that carry professional liability, a named engineer with regulatory authority is the signatory — never the platform, never the AI. The chain of responsibility is preserved end-to-end.

§ 02Data & privacyHow customer data is handled

Data & privacy.

Engineering project data is among the most sensitive a firm holds — drawings under non-disclosure, client information under contract, and regulatory submissions where leakage carries real cost. Builtly handles it accordingly.

01
Ownership
§ Customer data

The customer owns the data. Always.

All project inputs, intermediate artefacts and final deliverables are the property of the customer who uploaded them. Builtly holds operational access to deliver the platform; ownership does not transfer. Data export is available on demand in the formats the platform already produces.

On contract termination the customer retains the right to export everything. Builtly retains operational logs and audit trails for the regulatory retention period agreed in the contract; everything beyond that is deleted.

02
Model training
§ Privacy

Customer project data is not used to train models.

Builtly operates a hybrid AI architecture. Proprietary engineering models are trained on engineering literature, public regulatory text and synthetic engineering datasets — never on customer project data. Third-party models from Anthropic and OpenAI are invoked through their standard APIs under data-protection terms that prohibit training on customer inputs. Customers who want their de-identified data to contribute to model improvement may opt in under a separate addendum, but the default is no training, no exception, across every model in the stack.

Customers who actively want their de-identified data to contribute to model improvement may opt in under a separate addendum — but the default and the practical operation is that customer data stays customer data.

03
Residency
§ Geographic

Regional data residency, configurable per tenant.

Customer data is stored and processed in the geographic region selected during onboarding. Cross-border processing happens only when explicitly enabled by the customer, and is logged in the audit trail. The default for customers in regulated industries is in-region storage and in-region processing.

For customers with disclosure obligations under climate, financial or public-procurement rules, the residency configuration becomes part of the regulatory record kept against each deliverable.

§ 03Security architectureHow the platform is built

Security architecture.

Security as a property of the architecture rather than a layer above it. Authentication, authorisation, encryption and isolation are built into the foundations on which the modules run — not configured after the fact.

04
Authentication
§ Identity

Enterprise SSO. Role-based access. Hardware-key support.

Authentication is delegated to enterprise SSO providers via SAML and OIDC. Local accounts are not the default in enterprise deployments. Role-based access controls determine what each authenticated user can see, do and sign. Hardware security keys are supported for high-assurance roles — particularly engineers authorised to attest deliverables.

Session management follows enterprise norms: configurable session length, forced re-authentication for sensitive operations, and full audit logging of authentication events.

05
Encryption
§ Crypto

Encryption at rest. Encryption in transit. Per-tenant keys.

Customer data is encrypted at rest with per-tenant key isolation and in transit with TLS 1.3. Key rotation follows automated schedules. Bring-your-own-key arrangements are available for customers with regulatory or commercial requirements for KMS independence.

Signed deliverables carry SHA-256 cryptographic signatures bound to the platform version and the signing engineer's identity — making post-hoc tampering detectable on the document itself, not just in platform logs.

06
Isolation
§ Tenant

Logical isolation by default. Dedicated infrastructure on request.

The default deployment model uses logical tenant isolation — separate data stores, separate access scopes, separate audit trails per customer, on shared infrastructure. For customers under elevated regulatory rules, dedicated single-tenant infrastructure is available as part of the platform-licence agreement.

AI inference is isolated per tenant: requests do not cross tenant boundaries, and model context is reset between requests. There is no shared inference state across customers.

§ 04Audit & traceabilityThe chain of custody

Audit & traceability.

For deliverables that an engineer signs and a client submits to a regulator, the chain of custody matters as much as the result. Every claim in every signed deliverable is traceable to the data and the compute behind it.

07
Chain of custody
§ Provenance

From raw input to signed output, on one trail.

Every deliverable carries a complete provenance record: the source files uploaded, the module version that processed them, the AI engine and model version used, the deterministic computations applied, the engineer who reviewed, and the timestamp at every step. The trail is immutable and can be re-run deterministically against the same inputs.

For regulatory examination, the trail is the answer to "how did this number get into this report?" — without requiring reconstruction or interpretation after the fact.

MutabilityImmutable · append-only

Standard: End-to-end provenance
08
Signature binding
§ Crypto

Cryptographic binding between document and signer.

When an engineer attests a deliverable, the signature is cryptographically bound to: the document content, the version of the platform that produced it, the inputs the platform consumed, and the engineer's identity. Any post-signature modification breaks the signature in a way that can be verified independently of the platform.

The signature artefact travels with the document — the receiving party can verify integrity without depending on Builtly remaining available. The verification mechanism is open-spec.

09
Forensic export
§ Disputes

Full forensic export available on request.

If a deliverable is challenged — in court, in regulatory examination, or by an insurer following an incident — the customer can request the complete forensic record for that deliverable: provenance, signature, all intermediate artefacts, and the model versions involved. Builtly retains the relevant data for the regulatory retention period applicable to the deliverable type.

The export is designed to be defensible: a third-party expert can examine the record, re-run the deterministic computations, and arrive at the same result. The audit trail is the answer, not just the supporting evidence for it.

§ 05Regulatory complianceWhere the platform sits in the regulatory chain

Regulatory compliance.

The platform is not the regulated party — the engineer who signs is. But the platform operates in close proximity to regulated work, and is built to support the compliance obligations that flow from that proximity.

10
Engineering codes
§ Standards

Building codes and engineering standards encoded as logic.

The regulation engine encodes building codes, Eurocode-family standards, fire-safety regulations, environmental standards and acoustic-class requirements as machine-readable logic — not as free-text instructions for a model to interpret. When a standard updates, the rule pack updates, and the change propagates to every module that depends on it.

Each module's output specifies the exact code version and rule-pack version applied — so the regulatory basis of every deliverable is unambiguous on the document itself.

11
Data protection
§ GDPR & equivalent

Data protection by design. DPA available.

The platform handles personal data only where strictly required for the engineering function — typically engineer identifiers, contact information, and project-team metadata. Customer-personal data outside this scope should not reach the platform; the upload interface and API surface are scoped accordingly.

A standard Data Processing Agreement is available for customers subject to GDPR or equivalent regional data-protection rules, and the platform supports the data-subject rights — access, correction, deletion — that those rules require.

Procurement

Vendor onboarding ready for enterprise procurement.

For customers running formal vendor-onboarding processes — security questionnaires, supplier-risk reviews, financial diligence — Builtly provides a standardised vendor-onboarding pack: the architecture summary, the security posture, the data-handling documentation, the certifications in progress, and named contacts for procurement diligence.
