§ 04Inquiry qualityWhat helps us respond well

What helps us respond.

The most useful inquiry is short, specific, and gets to the actual question or request quickly. We do not require any of the items below — but inquiries that include them tend to get more useful first responses.

  1. 01

    The actual question.

    One paragraph, ideally one sentence

    What do you need from us? A demo? A licence quote? Information for a story? An integration discussion? The first paragraph should answer this. We can pick up everything else from a short conversation, but the question itself should be in the email.

  2. 02

    Your situation.

    Two or three lines of context

    What is the firm, the role, the project, or the publication? We do not need a full backgrounder — two or three lines is enough to know whether the conversation is with a consultancy, a developer, a journalist or a candidate, and to respond at the right level of depth.

  3. 03

    The timeline.

    Urgency or absence of it

    When do you need a response, and when do you need a decision or output? Hard deadlines get prioritised. Inquiries with no urgency get the standard cadence above. Either way is fine — but knowing helps us sequence inboxes appropriately.

  4. 04

    The format.

    Email · call · written response

    If you prefer a written response, a 30-minute call, or a longer briefing, say so. The default is a written reply with a proposed next step. Calls are scheduled on request — and the calendar is open if a call is the right move.
